TIPS / WHAT I DID...

  • On the straight edge templates, I put talking points at every 1/2"; I would recommend putting a nail hole at every half inch as well.

Step 1 - Cutting the Cores  

20.1.1 – Using the rudder templates, cut the cores -  Note-The camber is reversed on the bottom template for the bottom winglets .       

20.1.2 – Shape the bottom winglet planform as shown in the plans

20.1.3 – Using the root and bottom template cut the lower winglets

20.1.4 – Round the bottom edge of the lower winglet to a pleasing shape

 

Step 2 - Glassing the Skins

20.2.1 – Carve the tip cap to a pleasing shape -        

20.2.2 – Glue the core remnants to the table, to make a jig for the rudder -        

20.2.3 – Ensure cores are level, then 2 ply UND and 1 ply BID on outboard side -        

20.2.4 – Install COM antenna -        

20.2.5 – Then 2 ply UND and 1 ply BID on inboard side -      

  

  

Step 3 - Jigging Upper Winglet to Wing

20.3.1 – Cut out winglet trim template (M-20) - 

20.3.2 – Using template, mark and cut the upper winglet - 

20.3.3 – Secure the wing, top side up, and mark the WPRP (winglet positioning reference point) - 

20.3.4 – Place winglet on top of wing and move around until the a, B, and C dimensions are achieved. Once positioned, mark the wing top skin at the inboard edge of the winglet.

20.3.5 – Remove the winglet and cut the wing all the way through along this mark.

20.3.6 – Bondo winglet into place and allow to cure.

 

 

Step 4 - Inside Layups

20.4.1 – Turn the wing over and remove foam locally where wing and winglet meet

20.4.2 – Carve block to fit between wing and winglet

20.4.3 - Lay up an 8 ply BID layup, of 4" x 12" tapes, at the wing and winglet joint. Use Flox and foam wedges to hold the tapes in position and fill the void.

20.4.4 – Apply 8 ply BID (2 plies aft of the winglet trailing edge) forward of the rudder conduit

20.4.5 – After partial cure, micro block A into position

 

Step 5 - Outside Layers

20.5.1 – Carve block A to a flat diagonal, then round edges at the wing and winglet

20.5.2 – Layup 2 plies BID and 7 plies UND per plans

20.5.3 – After cure turn wing assembly over, and layup 2 plies BID and 7 plies UND

20.5.4 – Once cured, remove the jigging that held winglet in place

 

 

 

Step 6 - Lower Winglet Attach

20.6.1 – Trim lower winglet to match upper winglet at the root

20.6.2 – Micro lower winglet in place and use BID tapes on inside and outside surfaces to attach

20.6.3 – Install wingtip light of your choice, making sure it is not aft of the position shown. If fairings are made, cover foam with 2 plies BID

 

 

 

Step 7 - Rudder

20.7.1 – Carefully mark rudder dimensions. Cut skins with razor saw, then foam with hacksaw blade, to remove rudder

20.7.2 – Set wing with leading edge down. Remove foam around the cutout

20.7.3 – Layup 3 plies BID over entire area, and add 2 extra plies along outside edge of hinge locations

20.7.4 – After cure, notch the edge of the hinge locations to recess the hinges

20.7.5 – Drill and fasten the hinges to the winglet. Trim the width of the hinges as required to fit

20.7.6 – Remove foam around the edges of the rudder and at the belhorn. Glass with 3 plies BID +2 extra plies at the hinge locations and the bell crank depressions.

20.7.7 – Install hinges and belhorn per plans

20.7.8 – Install the tube and hook system (plans page 20–6) near the bottom of this broader along the inboard skin

20.7.9 – Go to chapter 16 to finish rudder control system
