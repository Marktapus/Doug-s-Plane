Step 2 - Fabrication of NG-30's

13.2.1 � Transfer template to H100 PVC foam - IMPORTANT - Note the newsletter #86 changes that require you add 2" to the low spot, including a 1" radius at the leading corner, then reinforce with 2 plies UNI running fore-aft on both sides of each NG-30.  Many ways people do this.  I traced the drawings onto white paper, with the required modification, then used spray adhesive to glue the tracings to heavy paperboard to create a sturdy template without damaging my originals.  I didn't tranfer the drawings until after I had glassed the inside surface.  Also, I saved a piece of the waste material to use as F-5 that is needed in Step 4.      Step Done

13.2.3 � Out of order - Glass inside surface 4 plies BID, any orientation - Did this step first.  Glassed 1 oversized foam piece that would be large enough for both sides.  After cure, I cut it in two pieces.  At the same time, I also glassed a 4"x8", 15 ply BID to be used for hardpoints later in this section.       Step Done

13.2.4 � Out of order - Bind 2 pieces, interior sides facing each other, then cut NG-30 - I used drywall screws to connect the two pieces.  I put a screw through the MKNG-6 pivot point, and 1 through the uppermost NG51 hole.  Then I placed several along the perimeter of the NG-30 (These were outside the line, so I wouldn't compromise the finished piece).  Once the pieces were bound, I was able to cut the parts on my scroll saw.  I cut them a little generous of the line, then sanded them to final shape.  This creates two identical parts.  However, you do need to pay attention when sanding to make sure that the sides remain square.       Step Done

13.2.2 � Peel ply and glass MKNG-6 pivot area on outside surface - Cut the peel ply to the shape specified in Fig.5, then centered over the pivot hole.  I left the piece a little long so it would extend outside the NG-30.  Laid up 2 plies BID between waxed paper and used a template to cut the MKNG-6 reinforcement piece. Covered with peel ply and wax paper and weighted part down for cure.       Step Done

13.2.5 � Drill pilot holes at identified locations - Plans want you to use a drill press while pieces are connected to insure that holes are perpendicular.       Step Not Done

13.2.6 � Fabricate doublers - There are two doublers for each NG-30.  One doubler is a plywood block with 1/4" aluminum insert, the second is a 1/8" aluminum doubler.  I cut the plywood per plans dimensions, however I made the aluminum insert larger than plans (primarily because I had 1.5" stock of the 1/4" aluminum).  I also cut the inserts and plywood openings with a 45 degree beveled edge to add greater strength to the part.  The bevels allow more contact area between the wood and the aluminum, and prevents the aluminum from being able to be pulled through the plywood.  The top, foreward, and aft edges of the plywood are beveled for a smooth transition during glassing. I rounded the edges to make sure I didn't have problems getting the glass to lie down (I did the same on the 1/8" doubler). Click on the pictures to the left for a better view.  Once cut, the aluminum insert is floxed into the plywood and allowed to cure.  However, I floxed the inserts in place at the same time I floxed the plywood over the MKNG-6 pivot point.  Before floxing the pieces in place, I used a self-etching primer to coat/seal the aluminum pieces.     Step Done

13.2.7 � Flox plywood doubler in place over MKNG-6 pivot area - Per plans.  I drew reference lines onto the NG-30's to make the placement easier.  After floxing the doublers in place, I covered them with wax paper and carefully weighted them down with a brick.       Step Done

13.2.8 � Cut & prep holes for hardpoints - I made the 15 ply piece during Step 13.2.3.  The H100 PVC is not the friendliest material to shape.  I was going to route the holes, but didn't have one of the pieces to my RotoZip to put it back in the router mode.  So I just used the handheld tool to slowly remove the material.  Based on the measurements from Bernard Siu, I made my hardpoints a little oversized (1.75").  Since I plan to use the Wilhelmson actuator, I added the middle hardpoints on both NG-30's as opposed to the left only.        Step Not Done

13.2.9 � Flox 15 ply hardpoints in place,  glass outside surface, add second doubler and cover with glass - Rather than do this as one large step, I floxed the hardpoints and doublers in place and allowed them to cure before I laid down the 4 ply BID layup and 2 ply UNI reinforcement (per Newsletter #86).  I used sockets and bricks to maintain downward pressure on the hardpoints while they cured.       Step Not Done

  

Step 3 - Installing the Worm Drive Assembly - ** Deviation: I plan on using an electric actuator mechanism**

The use of electric actuators has become a common alteration, with many flying examples. The plans designed worm drive system is inherently simple and reliable. However, it does not allow for raising/lowering the nose with the plane loaded.  In addition, there has been some cases where the worm gear has stripped and required replacement/repair. There is a minor weight penalty for using the electric acuator systems, but improved reliabilty and strength.  The steps listed below do not follow the plans method, although some of them may be similar, or the same.

Step 4 - Installation of the Box Assembly on the Fuselage

13.4.1 � Cut F-0 out of foam - Plans give option on choice of foams, I used the H100 PVC.  Like with the NG-30's, I chose a piece that could be glassed oversized, then cut to shape after cure.      Step Done

13.4.2 � Glass F-0 - Plans have you glass the aft side with 1 ply-BID, no orientation.  I made this a 3 ply lay-up with 1 BID, and 2 UNI.  I arranged the glass for fibers placed at 45 degrees.   After cure, I cut the F-0 per plans.  I then prepped the piece for future lay-ups, and added center line and NG-30 references.     Step Done

13.4.3 � Attach NG-30's and tape all corners  - Plans have you complete this step while the aft side glass is still wet. I did not do this.    Step Not Done

13.4.4 � Cut F-5 out of foam - Plans intruct you to use the white 18# clark foam.  Since I had glassed remnants from my NG-30 cuts, I used the H100 PVC.  Also, the plans instruct you to cut this piece at 3.5"x7".  However, due to the Newsletter modification of NG-30, this piece really only needs to be ~ 3.5"x4".  I still cut it at 7" in case I missed something.       Step Done

13.4.5 � Glass F-5 - Plans have you glass the forward face with 2 ply-BID, no orientation.  Since this was a remnant from Step 13.2.4, it already was glassed with 4 plies BID. I was okay with the weight penalty.       Step Done

13.4.6 � Attach F-5 to NG-30's and tape all corners  - Plans have you complete this step while the forward face glass is still wet. I did not do this.    Step Not Done
